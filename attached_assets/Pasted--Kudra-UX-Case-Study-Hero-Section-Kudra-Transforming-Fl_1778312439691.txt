# Case Study

Kudra V2 was designed to transform fragmented logistics operations into a centralized trip management ecosystem. The platform evolved beyond simple vehicle tracking and introduced structured workflows for fleet managers, branch operators, and logistics teams.

The goal was to reduce operational dependency on calls and spreadsheets while improving visibility across trips, drivers, fuel usage, and fleet analytics.

The product focused on building a scalable enterprise workflow where every vehicle movement was tied to a structured operational trip.

---

# Problem Statement

Logistics operations were heavily dependent on manual coordination.

Fleet managers relied on:

* Phone calls
* WhatsApp communication
* Spreadsheets
* Paper-based tracking

This created multiple operational issues:

* No structured trip lifecycle
* Lack of trip visibility
* Poor coordination between branches
* No centralized fuel tracking
* Limited driver accountability
* No operational analytics

Vehicles were moving without proper operational context, making it difficult for teams to understand:

* Why a vehicle was moving
* Which trip it belonged to
* Who the assigned driver was
* Fuel usage during trips
* Trip completion status

---

# Product Vision

To create a centralized operational control system for logistics fleets that provides complete visibility into trip planning, dispatch, monitoring, and analytics.

Kudra V2 aimed to establish a workflow-first ecosystem where operations teams could manage logistics through structured trip lifecycles rather than fragmented communication.

# Core Workflow

## 1. Trip Creation

Fleet managers create a trip by:

* Selecting origin and destination
* Assigning drivers
* Assigning vehicles
* Selecting routes
* Using suggested frequent routes

---

## 2. Trip Dispatch

At dispatch, the system captures:

* Starting odometer
* Starting fuel level
* Dispatch confirmation
* Driver assignment confirmation

The trip status changes to:

### Dispatched

Notifications are automatically sent to operational branches.

---

## 3. Ongoing Monitoring

Fleet managers monitor:

* Vehicle movement
* Trip progress
* Refueling updates
* Driver halt activity
* Operational delays

---

## 4. Fuel Tracking

During the trip, operators can record:

* Refueling amount
* Fuel quantity
* Fuel cost
* Refueling locations

This data enables mileage and efficiency reporting.

---

## 5. Driver Halt Tracking

The system records:

* Halt locations
* Halt durations
* Halt reasons
* Waiting times

This improves operational accountability and driver payment calculations.

---

## 6. Trip Completion

At trip completion, the system captures:

* Ending odometer
* Ending fuel level
* Total fuel consumed
* Trip completion time

The platform automatically calculates:

* Distance traveled
* Vehicle mileage
* Trip duration
* Fuel efficiency

---

## 7. Reports & Analytics

The analytics system generates:

### Vehicle Reports

* Average mileage
* Fuel efficiency
* Vehicle utilization

### Driver Reports

* Driver efficiency
* Halt duration tracking
* Trip completion history

### Operational Reports

* Route performance
* Fleet utilization trends
* Frequent route analytics
